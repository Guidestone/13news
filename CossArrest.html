Toledo Man Arrested in Overnight Vandalism Spree Tied to 'Severe Case' of Trump Derangement Syndrome

Published: March 31, 2025 at 10:31 AM EDT | Updated: March 31, 2025 at 11:15 AM EDT
By Jennifer Hastings
BREAKING NEWS
Toledo police vehicle pictured on scene. (Source: WTVG)

A Toledo man is in custody following a politically charged rampage that left multiple properties vandalized across two neighborhoods late Monday night. Authorities say the suspect, identified as 30-year-old Brandon Coss, displayed what they are calling a “clear-cut case of Trump Derangement Syndrome,” or TDS—a term used to describe extreme emotional responses to President Donald J. Trump.

Witnesses report that Coss targeted homes displaying pro-Trump signs, flags, bumper stickers, and even a patriotic garden gnome. The spree reportedly began around 10:45 PM in the Old Orchard and Westgate neighborhoods and was described as “erratic but oddly thematic.”

“We’ve seen passionate political behavior before,” said Sgt. Megan Whitaker of the Toledo Police Department. “But this was something else entirely. This was methodical, unfiltered, and deeply personal.”

According to investigators, Coss’ social media pages provided key evidence of his motive. Police say his posts were peppered with profanity-laced statements aimed at the President and his supporters, escalating in intensity over the past month.

“This country is trash. Throw it out.” — Excerpt from a March 6 Facebook post by Coss

In another post dated March 13, Coss wrote: “Trump blows d**k. His supporters are some of the stupidest people in America… Hope y’all lose a lot from this.” Officers say these messages, coupled with the specific targeting of Trump-themed decorations, leave no doubt as to the political nature of the vandalism.

“He spray-painted ‘Orange Man Down’ across my fence,” said local resident Bob Jenkins, whose yard sign was among the first targeted. “Then he put a glitter sticker on my mailbox and ran. It was surreal.”

“He was always ranting on Nextdoor,” added neighbor Tina Martinez. “But I never thought he’d go after our yard signs! I woke up to see my entire lawn covered in glitter confetti.”

One of several vandalized signs discovered by neighbors. (Source: The Tennessean)

Coss is being held on a $10,000 bond at the Lucas County Jail and is scheduled for arraignment on April 3. Police jokingly referred to the charges as “reckless political commentary,” in addition to vandalism and trespassing.
